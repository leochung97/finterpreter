### Detailed Analysis of Business Segments

Tesla’s core business remains its EV lineup, including the Model 3, Model Y, and upcoming Model Q. The refreshed Model Y launched in March 2025, but demand in China and Europe has weakened, with deliveries in China declining by 4,000 units YoY in Q1 2025. Tesla’s market share is under threat from competitors like BYD and NIO, which offer more affordable and technologically advanced models.

**2. Energy Segment:**  
Tesla’s energy business, including Powerwall and solar products, continues to grow but remains a smaller contributor to overall revenue. Record energy storage deployments in Q4 2024 provided some relief, but margins remain tight.

**3. Robotics and AI:**  
Tesla is investing heavily in robotics, with plans to produce 1,000 Optimus humanoid robots per month by late 2025. However, these initiatives are unlikely to contribute meaningfully to revenue in the near term.

Tesla remains a dominant player in the EV market but faces growing competition from traditional automakers (e.g., Volkswagen, GM) and Chinese manufacturers (e.g., BYD, NIO). Additionally, geopolitical tensions and regulatory challenges in China are eroding Tesla’s competitive edge.
