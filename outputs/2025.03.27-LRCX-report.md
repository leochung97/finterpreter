### Recent Product Launches and Technological Advancements

1. Akara®: Introduced in February 2025, Akara is the industry's most advanced conductor etch technology. It delivers unmatched etch precision for 3D chipmaking, enabling the scaling of gate-all-around (GAA) transistors and advanced memory devices[13].

2. ALTUS® Halo: Unveiled in February 2025, ALTUS Halo is the world's first atomic layer deposition (ALD) tool that utilizes molybdenum in leading-edge semiconductor production. This innovation marks a new era in semiconductor metallization[18].

3. Dry Photoresist Technology: Qualified for 28nm pitch back-end-of-line logic at 2nm and below, enhancing extreme ultraviolet (EUV) lithography's resolution, productivity, and yield[8].

These technological advancements position Lam Research at the forefront of semiconductor manufacturing innovation, supporting its growth in high-value market segments.

### Market Position and Competitive Landscape

Lam Research holds a strong position in the semiconductor equipment industry, with a particular focus on etch, deposition, and clean technologies. As of Q2 2024, the company had a 7.52% market share in the industrial machinery and components industry[14].

Key competitors include:
- Applied Materials (13.55% market share)
- ASML (15.57% market share)

Lam Research's focus on its "4-Horsemen" technologies (Gate All Around, Backside Power, Advanced Packaging, and Dry Resist) is expected to drive growth rates exceeding the overall Wafer Fabrication Equipment (WFE) market expansion[14].

### Forward-Looking Guidance and Management Commentary

During the most recent earnings call, management provided the following guidance and commentary:

- Fiscal 2025 EPS forecast: $3.50 (up 17.1% from fiscal 2024)
- Fiscal 2026 EPS forecast: $3.94 (up 12.6% YoY)
- WFE market expected to grow to approximately $100 billion in 2025 (mid-single-digit increase from 2024)
- Lam Research anticipates outperforming market growth, driven by demand for NAND upgrades and benefits from leading-edge nodes like Gate-All-Around (GAA) and Advanced Packaging[9]

Management expressed optimism about opportunities in the NAND market, where Lam Research claims a 30+% share of wallet on capacity upgrades[9].
