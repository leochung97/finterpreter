## Investment Summary

We maintain an OUTPERFORM rating on Advanced Micro Devices, Inc. (AMD) with a 12-month price target of $205, representing a 95% upside from the current price of $105.09. AMD's strong positioning in the AI and data center markets, coupled with its expanding product portfolio and strategic acquisitions, positions the company for significant growth in 2025 and beyond.

## Key Financial Metrics and Recent Performance

- Q4 2024 Revenue: $7.7 billion (record), up 24% year-over-year
- Q4 2024 Gross Margin: 51% GAAP, 54% non-GAAP
- Q4 2024 Operating Income: $871 million GAAP, $2.0 billion non-GAAP (record)
- Q4 2024 Net Income: $482 million GAAP, $1.8 billion non-GAAP (record)
- Q4 2024 Diluted EPS: $0.29 GAAP, $1.09 non-GAAP
- Full Year 2024 Revenue: $25.8 billion (record)
- Full Year 2024 Gross Margin: 49% GAAP, 53% non-GAAP (record)
- Full Year 2024 Operating Income: $1.9 billion GAAP, $6.1 billion non-GAAP
- Full Year 2024 Net Income: $1.6 billion GAAP, $5.4 billion non-GAAP
- Full Year 2024 Diluted EPS: $1.00 GAAP, $3.31 non-GAAP

## Detailed Analysis of Business Segments

### Data Center

- Q4 2024 Revenue: $3.9 billion (record), up 69% year-over-year
- Full Year 2024 Revenue: $12.6 billion (record), up 94% year-over-year
- Growth driven by strong ramp of AMD Instinct GPU shipments and increased EPYC CPU sales
- AMD delivered over $5 billion in AMD Instinct accelerator revenue in 2024

### Client

- Q4 2024 Revenue: $2.3 billion (record), up 58% year-over-year
- Full Year 2024 Revenue: $7.1 billion (record), up 52% year-over-year
- Growth attributed to strong demand for AMD Ryzen processors in desktop and mobile markets

### Gaming

- Q4 2024 Revenue: $563 million, down 59% year-over-year
- Full Year 2024 Revenue: $2.6 billion, down 58% year-over-year
- Decline primarily due to decreased semi-custom revenue

### Embedded

- Q4 2024 Revenue: $923 million, down 13% year-over-year
- Full Year 2024 Revenue: $3.6 billion, down 33% year-over-year
- Decline attributed to customers normalizing inventory levels

## Recent Product Launches and Technological Advancements

- Ryzen AI Max Series: Up to 16 "Zen 5" CPU cores, RDNA 3.5 graphics, and XDNA 2 NPUs delivering 50 TOPS for AI processing
- Ryzen AI 300 Series: Mainstream AI-capable processors
- Ryzen 200 Series: Focused on everyday productivity
- Ryzen 9 9950X3D and 9900X3D CPUs: Second-generation 3D V-Cache technology, up to 16 cores and 5.7 GHz boost speeds
- Radeon RX 9070 GPUs: Based on RDNA 4 architecture, featuring AI-powered FidelityFX Super Resolution 4
- Ryzen Z2 Series: Enhanced efficiency for handheld gaming devices
- Ryzen AI Max PRO Series: Enterprise-grade security and AI performance for commercial solutions

## Market Position and Competitive Landscape

AMD continues to gain market share in the CPU market, challenging Intel's dominance. In the AI and GPU markets, AMD is positioning itself as a strong competitor to NVIDIA, particularly with its MI300 series accelerators. The company's strategic focus on AI and data center markets has resulted in significant revenue growth and increased adoption of its EPYC processors and Instinct accelerators.

AMD's partnerships with major cloud providers like IBM, Microsoft Azure, and Google Cloud strengthen its position in the AI and data center markets. The acquisition of ZT Systems for $4.9 billion enhances AMD's ability to offer complete AI solutions, including hardware, software, and system-level integration.

## Forward-Looking Guidance and Management Commentary

For Q1 2025, AMD expects:
- Revenue of approximately $7.1 billion (±$300 million)
- Non-GAAP gross margin of approximately 54%

Management expects continued growth opportunities based on the strength of AMD's product portfolio and increasing demand for high-performance and adaptive computing. The company projects the PC TAM to grow by a mid-single-digit percentage year-over-year in 2025 and believes it can grow client segment revenue well ahead of the market.

## Valuation Analysis and Price Target Justification

Our $205 price target is based on a forward P/E multiple of 40x applied to our 2025 EPS estimate of $5.11. This valuation is justified by:

1. AMD's strong position in high-growth markets (AI, data center)
2. Expected earnings growth of 54% in 2025
3. Expanding market share in CPUs and potential in AI accelerators
4. Strategic acquisitions and partnerships enhancing long-term growth prospects

## Key Risks and Opportunities

Risks:
- Intense competition from NVIDIA and Intel
- Dependence on TSMC for chip fabrication
- Potential economic slowdowns affecting demand
- Execution risks in new markets like AI

Opportunities:
- Expanding AI and data center market share
- Continued gains in CPU market share
- Growth in 5G and edge computing markets
- Synergies from strategic acquisitions and partnerships
